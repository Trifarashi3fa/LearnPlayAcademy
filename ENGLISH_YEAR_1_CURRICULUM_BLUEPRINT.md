## Curriculum Principles

- Keep language at Year 1 level: short words, short sentences, familiar contexts.
- Teach one skill per question.
- Use visuals to support comprehension, not decorate the screen.
- Keep each explanation focused on the tested skill.
- Use the approved six English question types only.
- Keep English content separate from Mathematics content and progress identity.
- Do not use unsupported interactions such as drag and drop, sorting, or puzzle mechanics unless the engine fully supports them later.

## Alignment Guidance

English Year 1 content should align with early primary skills: letter and sound recognition, familiar vocabulary, simple reading, classroom communication, and basic grammar awareness through examples.

Where Cambridge Primary alignment is used, keep it to early-stage outcomes such as recognising letters and simple words, understanding short phrases, and using basic vocabulary in familiar contexts.

The app must not claim formal certification or compliance unless reviewed externally.

## Level Standards

### Level 1: Alphabet Recognition

- Learning objective: Recognise letters and match uppercase to lowercase forms.
- Age suitability: Early Year 1 and learners who need alphabet reinforcement.
- Difficulty progression: Single letters, uppercase/lowercase pairs, then near-shape distractors.
- Explanation style: Show the letter pair and say they belong to the same letter family.
- LearnBot guidance: Encourage looking at letter shape and saying the letter name.
- Visual requirement: Large letters, simple letter cards, no busy background.
- Assessment objective: Confirm that the learner can identify letters without guessing from position.

### Level 2: Letter Sounds and Phonics

- Learning objective: Connect common beginning sounds with letters and familiar words.
- Difficulty progression: Obvious first sounds, then similar sounds such as b/p or m/n.
- Explanation style: Say the word slowly and highlight the first sound.
- LearnBot guidance: Prompt learners to listen to the first sound.
- Visual requirement: Clear picture plus first-letter focus.
- Assessment objective: Confirm that the learner can hear and choose beginning sounds.

### Level 3: CVC Words

- Learning objective: Blend and complete simple consonant-vowel-consonant words.
- Difficulty progression: cat, sun, pen style words before less familiar words.
- Explanation style: Break word into sounds, then blend.
- LearnBot guidance: Encourage saying each sound slowly.
- Visual requirement: One clear picture per target word when helpful.
- Assessment objective: Confirm early decoding and spelling pattern recognition.

### Level 4: Everyday Vocabulary

- Learning objective: Recognise familiar nouns, verbs, and classroom words.
- Difficulty progression: picture-word matching, then meaning selection.
- Explanation style: Give the word, meaning, and one simple example.
- LearnBot guidance: Connect the word to the picture or daily use.
- Visual requirement: Clear object/action images with no hidden ambiguity.
- Assessment objective: Confirm word meaning in familiar contexts.

### Level 5: Simple Spelling

- Learning objective: Choose or build the correctly spelled word.
- Difficulty progression: one-letter distractors, then letter order errors.
- Explanation style: Highlight the correct spelling and one spelling clue.
- LearnBot guidance: Encourage checking every letter from left to right.
- Visual requirement: Word cards, letter tiles, optional picture clue.
- Assessment objective: Confirm recognition of correct spelling.

### Level 6: Grammar Basics

- Learning objective: Recognise question, statement, command, noun, verb, and basic punctuation.
- Difficulty progression: sentence type by punctuation, then word role by meaning.
- Explanation style: Use one short rule and one example.
- LearnBot guidance: Point to punctuation or action words.
- Visual requirement: Sentence cards and simple labels.
- Assessment objective: Confirm early grammar awareness without long terminology.

### Level 7: Reading Simple Sentences

- Learning objective: Read a short sentence and understand the key meaning.
- Difficulty progression: one-clause sentences before longer simple sentences.
- Explanation style: Identify who or what the sentence is about.
- LearnBot guidance: Read the sentence slowly and look for the action word.
- Visual requirement: One supportive picture when needed.
- Assessment objective: Confirm sentence-level comprehension.

### Level 8: Reading Comprehension

- Learning objective: Answer a simple question from a short sentence, mini passage, or picture.
- Difficulty progression: direct facts before simple inference.
- Explanation style: Point to the clue in the text or picture.
- LearnBot guidance: Look back at the sentence before choosing.
- Visual requirement: Picture scenes must be simple and unambiguous.
- Assessment objective: Confirm understanding beyond single words.

### Level 9: Everyday Communication

- Learning objective: Choose simple everyday phrases for classroom, family, and polite communication.
- Difficulty progression: greetings, requests, thanks, simple question responses.
- Explanation style: Explain when the phrase is used.
- LearnBot guidance: Think about what someone should say in that situation.
- Visual requirement: Friendly everyday scenes.
- Assessment objective: Confirm practical communication meaning.

### Level 10: English Explorer Challenge

- Learning objective: Review alphabet, phonics, vocabulary, spelling, grammar, sentences, and communication.
- Difficulty progression: mixed but fair, with no new skills introduced.
- Explanation style: Quick recap of the tested skill.
- LearnBot guidance: Celebrate progress and remind the learner of the strategy.
- Visual requirement: Challenge badge, English Explorer theme, clear skill visuals.
- Assessment objective: Confirm readiness for post-beta English expansion.

## Source Question Pool Standard

- Recommended source pool: 24 to 40 reviewed questions per level.
- Active session: 10 questions.
- Question type mix: vary by level, but always fit the skill.
- No repeated wording groups inside the same active session.
- No question may reveal the answer through the hint, image filename, visual label, or option order.

## Production Content Gate

Before activation, each English question must have a unique question ID, locked level and subject identity, supported type A to F, verified correct answer, short explanation, LearnBot tip, voice script where relevant, visual description, and resolved QA notes.
