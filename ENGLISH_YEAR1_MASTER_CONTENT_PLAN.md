# English Year 1 Master Content Plan

## Scope

This plan defines the complete English Year 1 Forest World curriculum for 10 standardized levels. English remains inactive until the imported and reviewed content is approved, validated, and connected to a future English manifest.

## Package Identity

- Subject: English
- Year: 1
- World: forest-world
- Display name: English Forest World
- Status: Review content only
- Source questions: 300
- Target active gameplay questions later: 100 total, 10 per level
- Question status in generated CSV files: Review
- Supported question types only: Multiple Choice, Match Pairs, Tap Correct, Fill Missing Letter, Fill Missing Word, Text Input, True / False

## Curriculum Principles

- Use short prompts suitable for Year 1 learners.
- Teach visually before adding longer text.
- Keep explanations to one clear idea.
- Use LearnBot tips as coaching, not as long instruction.
- Use voice scripts to model pronunciation and reading rhythm.
- Keep unsupported future interaction types out of this source bank.
- Keep all English content isolated from Mathematics until activation.

## Alignment Notes

- KSSR alignment is written at a practical Year 1 skill level and should be reviewed by an English curriculum reviewer before approval.
- Cambridge alignment references Cambridge Primary English Stage 1 early literacy skills and should be reviewed before public launch.
- No legal, standards, or assessment claim should be published without final education review.

## Forest L01: Alphabet Recognition

**Learning Objectives**

- Recognise uppercase and lowercase letters.
- Match uppercase letters to lowercase letters.
- Identify letters in a simple alphabet sequence.

**KSSR Alignment**

- KSSR Year 1 English: recognise and name letters of the alphabet and connect letters to early reading.

**Cambridge Primary Alignment**

- Cambridge Primary English Stage 1: identify letters, print concepts, and simple alphabet knowledge.

**Difficulty Progression**

- Starts with single letters, then matches uppercase/lowercase, then completes short letter sequences.

**Vocabulary List**

- A, B, C, D, E, F, M, P, S, T, a, b, c, m, s, t

**Learning Outcomes**

- Children can point to named letters.
- Children can match uppercase and lowercase forms.
- Children can complete simple alphabet patterns.

**Recommended Question Mix**

- 6 Multiple Choice, 5 Match Pairs, 5 Tap Correct, 4 Fill Missing Letter, 5 Text Input, 5 True / False.
- Generated source question mix: Multiple Choice: 6; Match Pairs: 5; Tap Correct: 5; Fill Missing Letter: 4; Text Input: 5; True / False: 5.
- Later gameplay recommendation: select 10 questions using a balanced mix from the 30-question source pool.

**Assessment Objectives**

- Check alphabet recognition, uppercase/lowercase matching, and sequence confidence.

**LearnBot Teaching Approach**

- Use short prompts: look at the shape, say the letter, then choose the match.

**Voice Narration Style**

- Slow, clear letter names with pauses after each letter.

**Visual Requirements**

- Large letter cards, uppercase/lowercase pairs, simple alphabet path cards.

**Teacher Notes**

- Keep the focus on recognition before handwriting. Avoid overloading children with the full alphabet at once.

**Parent Notes**

- Ask your child to find the same letter on books, signs, or labels at home.

## Forest L02: Letter Sounds (Phonics)

**Learning Objectives**

- Recognise common beginning sounds.
- Connect a beginning sound to a familiar word.
- Listen for the first sound in simple words.

**KSSR Alignment**

- KSSR Year 1 English: listen to and recognise initial sounds in familiar words.

**Cambridge Primary Alignment**

- Cambridge Primary English Stage 1: use phonics to connect sounds and letters in simple words.

**Difficulty Progression**

- Starts with one sound and picture, then selects words by beginning sound, then supplies the first letter.

**Vocabulary List**

- apple, ball, cat, dog, fish, moon, pen, sun, tiger, van

**Learning Outcomes**

- Children can identify the first sound in familiar words.
- Children can match sound-letter-word relationships.
- Children can say a short phonics clue aloud.

**Recommended Question Mix**

- 6 Multiple Choice, 5 Match Pairs, 5 Tap Correct, 5 Fill Missing Letter, 4 Text Input, 5 True / False.
- Generated source question mix: Multiple Choice: 6; Match Pairs: 5; Tap Correct: 5; Fill Missing Letter: 5; Text Input: 4; True / False: 5.
- Later gameplay recommendation: select 10 questions using a balanced mix from the 30-question source pool.

**Assessment Objectives**

- Check whether children hear and identify beginning sounds.

**LearnBot Teaching Approach**

- Ask children to say the first sound before choosing.

**Voice Narration Style**

- Emphasise the beginning sound only: /b/ ... ball.

**Visual Requirements**

- Picture cards for familiar objects with a highlighted first letter.

**Teacher Notes**

- Model sounds, not only letter names. Keep /c/ as hard c for cat.

**Parent Notes**

- Play 'What sound starts this word?' with safe everyday objects.

## Forest L03: Match Word to Picture

**Learning Objectives**

- Match a word to a picture.

**Cambridge Primary Alignment**

- Cambridge Primary English Stage 1: read simple words and connect print to meaning.

**Difficulty Progression**

- Starts with clear one-word labels, then chooses between similar short words.

**Vocabulary List**

- cat, dog, sun, bus, hat, bag, cup, fish, bird, tree

**Learning Outcomes**

- Children can identify a familiar word.
- Children can connect the printed word to a picture.
- Children can reject similar but incorrect words.

**Recommended Question Mix**

- 6 Multiple Choice, 6 Match Pairs, 5 Tap Correct, 4 Fill Missing Word, 4 Text Input, 5 True / False.

**Assessment Objectives**

- Check word recognition and picture-word meaning.

**LearnBot Teaching Approach**

**Voice Narration Style**

**Visual Requirements**

**Teacher Notes**

**Parent Notes**

- Ask your child to name objects and find matching labels.

## Forest L04: CVC Words

**Learning Objectives**

- Blend three-letter CVC words.

**KSSR Alignment**

**Cambridge Primary Alignment**

**Difficulty Progression**

**Vocabulary List**

- cat, bat, mat, pen, hen, sun, run, cup, bug, fox

**Learning Outcomes**

- Children can blend simple sounds into words.
- Children can identify a missing letter in a CVC word.
- Children can type or choose a simple CVC word.

**Recommended Question Mix**

- Later gameplay recommendation: select 10 questions using a balanced mix from the 30-question source pool.

**Assessment Objectives**

- Check blending, short-vowel recognition, and spelling readiness.

**LearnBot Teaching Approach**

- Guide children to say each sound, then blend the word.

**Voice Narration Style**

- Segment and blend: /c/ /a/ /t/ ... cat.

**Visual Requirements**

- CVC word cards, sound boxes, and simple object pictures.

**Teacher Notes**

- Do not rush spelling. Let children hear and blend first.

**Parent Notes**

- Practise short words by stretching sounds gently.
