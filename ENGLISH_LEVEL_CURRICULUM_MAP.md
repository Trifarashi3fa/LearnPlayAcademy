# English Forest World Level Curriculum Map

## Scope

This curriculum map defines a free beta English Year 1 Forest World with 5 levels and 50 active gameplay questions. It is intended as a safe multi-subject beta expansion, not a full English course.

Recommended content volume:

- 5 levels
- 30 to 50 source questions per level
- 10 approved active gameplay questions per level
- 50 active questions total
- Free access during beta

Curriculum alignment note:

The KSSR and Cambridge mappings below are working alignment descriptions, not final standard-code mappings. Exact standard codes should be verified by a curriculum reviewer before questions are marked Approved.

## Level 1: Uppercase and Lowercase Letters

Learning objective:

- Recognize uppercase and lowercase versions of common letters.
- Match a capital letter to its small letter.
- Build early alphabet confidence.

KSSR working alignment:

- Year 1 English early literacy.
- Letter recognition, alphabet awareness, and early print awareness.
- Supports CEFR-aligned beginning literacy foundations used in KSSR English.

Cambridge working alignment:

- Cambridge Primary Stage 1 English or English as a Second Language early reading foundation.
- Letter recognition, alphabet knowledge, and early word/print awareness.

Age suitability:

- Year 1 students, ages approximately 6-7.
- Also suitable for early readers who need alphabet reinforcement.

Supported question types:

- Multiple Choice
- Match Pairs
- Tap Correct Answer
- Fill Missing Letter
- True or False

Recommended source questions:

- 30-40 rows.
- Include at least 20 letter-pair items and 10 mixed review items.

Recommended active questions:

- 10 active questions.

Difficulty progression:

1. Match visually distinct pairs, such as A/a, M/m, T/t.
2. Match less visually obvious pairs, such as E/e, R/r, G/g.
3. Avoid high-confusion letters until students have confidence.
4. Add review with b/d and p/q only as late-level challenge if visuals are clear.

Explanation style:

- Show a large uppercase letter and lowercase letter.
- Use one short teaching idea:
  - "Big A and small a are the same letter."
  - "The letter shape changes, but the letter name stays the same."

LearnBot guidance style:

- Encourage visual comparison.
- Example:
  - "Look at the shape first."
  - "Say the letter name out loud."
  - "Big letter and small letter are partners."

Voice narration requirements:

- Read the letter name clearly.
- Optional: say "uppercase A" and "lowercase a".
- Avoid long narration.

Visual asset requirements:

- Letter tile components for uppercase and lowercase.
- Forest signpost or leaf-shaped letter cards.
- No large image files required for the core interaction.

Assessment objective:

- Student can match uppercase and lowercase letters with at least 80% accuracy.

Sample question patterns:

- "Which small letter matches A?"
- "Tap the letter that matches m."
- "True or False: B and b are the same letter."
- "Fill the missing lowercase letter: T -> _"

## Level 2: Beginning Sounds

Learning objective:

- Identify the beginning sound of simple familiar words.
- Match a picture or word to its first letter sound.

KSSR working alignment:

- Year 1 English phonics foundation.
- Sound-letter relationship and early listening/reading skills.
- Supports early decoding readiness.

Cambridge working alignment:

- Cambridge Primary Stage 1 phonics and early reading.
- Recognize sounds in spoken words and connect sounds to letters.

Age suitability:

- Year 1 students, ages approximately 6-7.
- Best for learners who have completed Level 1 or already know basic letters.

Supported question types:

- Multiple Choice
- Tap Correct Answer
- Fill Missing Letter
- True or False
- Match Pairs, only where sound/picture pairs are clear.

Recommended source questions:

- 40-50 rows.
- Include multiple examples for each target sound.

Recommended active questions:

- 10 active questions.

Difficulty progression:

1. Start with clear initial consonants: m, s, t, p, b, c.
2. Use familiar picture words: sun, cat, dog, pen, bag, ball.
3. Avoid ambiguous pronunciation or blends in beta.
4. End with mixed beginning sound review.

Explanation style:

- Show the picture, word, and first letter.
- Keep steps short:
  - "Say the word."
  - "Listen to the first sound."
  - "Choose the letter that makes that sound."

LearnBot guidance style:

- Prompt oral repetition.
- Example:
  - "Say the word slowly."
  - "Listen to the first sound."
  - "The first sound helps you choose."

Voice narration requirements:

- Say the word naturally.
- Repeat the first sound clearly.
- Do not overuse phonetic notation on student screens.

Visual asset requirements:

- Clear picture cards for familiar nouns.
- Optional sound bubble or first-letter highlight.
- Use culturally neutral, child-friendly images.

Assessment objective:

- Student can identify common beginning sounds in familiar words.

Sample question patterns:

- "What sound does cat start with?"
- "Tap the picture that starts with /s/."
- "Fill the first letter: _at."
- "True or False: dog starts with d."

## Level 3: Match Words to Pictures

Learning objective:

- Read familiar words and match them to pictures.
- Strengthen vocabulary recognition.

KSSR working alignment:

- Year 1 English vocabulary and early reading.
- Recognizing familiar words and connecting print to meaning.

Cambridge working alignment:

- Cambridge Primary Stage 1 reading and vocabulary development.
- Understand simple words supported by visuals.

Age suitability:

- Year 1 students, ages approximately 6-7.
- Suitable after basic letter and sound practice.

Supported question types:

- Match Pairs
- Tap Correct Answer
- Multiple Choice
- True or False

Recommended source questions:

- 40-60 rows.
- Include word-picture pair sets and individual picture-choice questions.

Recommended active questions:

- 10 active questions.

Difficulty progression:

1. Single word to one picture.
2. Picture to one word from three choices.
3. Match two or three word-picture pairs.
4. Review with similar distractors such as cat/dog, bag/book.

Explanation style:

- Show the picture and word together.
- Use direct language:
  - "This picture is a cat."
  - "The word cat matches the picture."

LearnBot guidance style:

- Focus on print-to-picture connection.
- Example:
  - "Look at the picture first."
  - "Read the word slowly."
  - "Match the word to what you see."

Voice narration requirements:

- Read each word clearly.
- Optionally say "This word is cat."
- Avoid long sentences.

Visual asset requirements:

- Picture cards for 20-30 beginner vocabulary words.
- Word cards using large readable type.
- Match Pairs layout should have clear left and right columns.

Assessment objective:

- Student can match common Year 1 vocabulary words to pictures.

Sample question patterns:

- "Which word matches the picture?"
- "Tap the picture for sun."
- "Match the words to the pictures."
- "True or False: This is a dog."

## Level 4: Simple CVC Words

Learning objective:

- Read and complete simple consonant-vowel-consonant words.
- Recognize short vowel word patterns.

KSSR working alignment:

- Year 1 English phonics and word reading.
- Supports early decoding and spelling of simple words.

Cambridge working alignment:

- Cambridge Primary Stage 1 phonics and early word reading.
- Blend and read simple regular words.

Age suitability:

- Year 1 students, ages approximately 6-7.
- Best for learners comfortable with letters and beginning sounds.

Supported question types:

- Fill Missing Letter
- Multiple Choice
- Tap Correct Answer
- True or False
- Match Pairs for word-picture pairs.

Recommended source questions:

- 50-60 rows.
- Include short-a, short-e, short-i, short-o, and short-u examples only where age-appropriate.

Recommended active questions:

- 10 active questions.

Difficulty progression:

1. Complete first letter: _at.
2. Complete final letter: ca_.
3. Complete middle vowel: c_t.
4. Choose the word that matches the picture.
5. Mixed CVC review.

Explanation style:

- Show letter boxes.
- Highlight the missing letter.
- Keep steps short:
  - "Say the word."
  - "Listen for the missing sound."
  - "Put the letter in the box."

LearnBot guidance style:

- Encourage blending.
- Example:
  - "Say each sound, then blend."
  - "Look at the picture to help."
  - "The missing sound tells you the letter."

Voice narration requirements:

- Say the full word.
- Optional: segment sounds slowly, such as c-a-t.
- Keep narration short and supportive.

Visual asset requirements:

- Letter boxes.
- Word cards.
- Picture cards for each CVC word.
- Highlight treatment for missing letters.

Assessment objective:

- Student can complete and read simple CVC words with visual support.

Sample question patterns:

- "Fill the missing letter: c_t."
- "Which word says cat?"
- "Tap the word that matches the picture."
- "True or False: pen has three letters."

## Level 5: Vocabulary and Simple Sentences

Learning objective:

- Read short familiar words in simple sentence contexts.
- Choose the word or picture that completes meaning.

KSSR working alignment:

- Year 1 English basic reading comprehension.
- Recognizing familiar vocabulary in simple phrases and sentences.

Cambridge working alignment:

- Cambridge Primary Stage 1 reading comprehension and vocabulary use.
- Understand short simple texts with support from pictures.

Age suitability:

- Year 1 students, ages approximately 6-7.
- Best as a beta capstone after Levels 1-4.

Supported question types:

- Multiple Choice
- Fill Missing Word
- Tap Correct Answer
- True or False
- Match Pairs for sentence-picture or word-picture review.

Recommended source questions:

- 50-70 rows.
- Include simple high-frequency sentence frames.

Recommended active questions:

- 10 active questions.

Difficulty progression:

1. Choose a word to complete a simple phrase.
2. Match a short sentence to a picture.
3. True/False sentence-picture checks.
4. Fill a missing familiar word.
5. Review all beta skills in simple context.

Explanation style:

- Show the picture and sentence together.
- Highlight the target word.
- Keep steps short:
  - "Look at the picture."
  - "Read the sentence."
  - "Choose the word that makes sense."

LearnBot guidance style:

- Encourage meaning-making.
- Example:
  - "Use the picture clue."
  - "Read the sentence slowly."
  - "Choose the word that fits."

Voice narration requirements:

- Read the full sentence clearly.
- Repeat the target word if needed.
- Avoid long grammar explanations.

Visual asset requirements:

- Sentence cards.
- Picture scenes for simple sentence meaning.
- Word tiles.
- Highlighted answer tile.

Assessment objective:

- Student can understand simple familiar words and sentences with picture support.

Sample question patterns:

- "I see a ___."
- "Which picture matches: The cat is big?"
- "True or False: The sentence says, I see a sun."
- "Tap the word that completes the sentence."

## Beta Source Pool Recommendation

| Level | Source Pool | Active Questions | Reason |
| --- | ---: | ---: | --- |
| 1 | 30-40 | 10 | Enough alphabet variety for replay without overwhelming first release |

| 4 | 50-60 | 10 | CVC words need controlled vowel and distractor coverage |
| 5 | 50-70 | 10 | Simple sentences need more variation and review |

Total recommended beta source pool:

- Minimum: 210 source questions
- Better: 250 source questions
- Active beta gameplay: 50 questions

## Explanation and LearnBot Standards

Every English question should include:

- One clear explanation, no long paragraphs.
- Three maximum teaching steps.
- One LearnBot tip, maximum two short lines.
- Voice script that can be read by a friendly narrator.
- Visual description that names the exact asset or letter/word tile shown.

Do:

- Use "Say the word slowly."
- Use "Look at the first letter."
- Use "Match the word to the picture."
- Use "Read the sentence and check the picture."

Do not:

- Use grammar-heavy explanations.
- Use long phonics terminology on student screens.
- Use unfamiliar vocabulary as distractors.
- Use unsupported future interaction types in active content.

## Assessment Design Objectives

English beta assessment can be added later. For now, every level should tag assessment intent:

- Level 1: Can match uppercase/lowercase letters.
- Level 2: Can identify beginning sounds.
- Level 3: Can match familiar words to pictures.
- Level 4: Can complete simple CVC words.
- Level 5: Can understand simple vocabulary and sentence meaning.

Recommended future assessment:

- Free English Check: 5 questions, one per level.
- Full English Forest Assessment: 20 questions, mixed skills.
- Parent report:
  - Letter confidence
  - Sound awareness
  - Word-picture recognition
  - CVC decoding
  - Simple sentence understanding
